## Analysis

NVIDIA's Q2 FY2026 earnings report, due after market close today, is set against a backdrop of a newly implemented 15% US revenue-sharing agreement on specific AI chip sales to China. This unprecedented deal, confirmed in August 2025, allows NVIDIA to resume exports of its H20 chips to the crucial Chinese market, which had been largely restricted. While the agreement reopens a significant revenue stream, its financial implications, particularly on gross margins, present a complex and potentially non-consensus picture for investors.

Analysts are closely scrutinizing how the 15% levy, applied directly to topline revenue from these China sales, will impact NVIDIA's profitability. Estimates suggest a potential 5-15 percentage point reduction on gross margins for China-bound chips, with some analysts forecasting a one to four percentage point hit to NVIDIA's overall gross margins. This comes after Q1 FY2026 saw a notable decline in gross margins to 60.5% due to a $4.5 billion charge related to H20 inventory write-offs caused by the initial export ban. NVIDIA has previously guided for a recovery to approximately 71.8% gross margin in Q2, aiming for the mid-70% range by year-end, making the actual reported Q2 gross margin a critical metric to watch.

A key area of non-consensus revolves around the actual volume of China sales that will materialize under this new arrangement. While the deal theoretically enables the recovery of up to $8 billion in previously lost H20 revenue for Q2, some analysts did not factor these sales into their forecasts due to the late approval and reported "pushback" from Chinese authorities urging domestic firms to reduce purchases of U.S. chips. This divergence in analyst expectations creates potential for either a positive surprise if sales exceed conservative estimates or a negative one if Chinese demand remains subdued. The "unusual" nature of this revenue-sharing model, which critics have labeled a "creative taxation scheme," also raises questions about its long-term implications for U.S. tech companies operating in regulated markets. Investors will be seeking detailed commentary from management on the timing, materiality, and expected gross margin implications of this agreement during the earnings call.
