## Analysis

NVIDIA (NVDA) is poised to report its Q2 FY2026 earnings today, August 27, 2025, after market close. While the data center segment remains the primary revenue driver, recent developments in the automotive and robotics segments indicate significant acceleration and increasing market penetration for NVIDIA's DRIVE platform. This growth is underpinned by strong adoption of NVIDIA's next-generation autonomous vehicle (AV) technologies and strategic partnerships.

A key development is the imminent availability of the NVIDIA DRIVE AGX Thor developer kit for pre-order, with deliveries expected in September. This platform, built on the Blackwell architecture and Arm Neoverse V3AE CPUs, is already seeing widespread adoption by major automotive original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) like BYD, GAC, IM Motors, Li Auto, Volvo Cars, Xiaomi, and Zeekr. Additionally, autonomous trucking companies such as Aurora, Gatik, PlusAI, and Waabi, along with AV partners DeepRoute.ai, Nuro, WeRide, and ZYT, are leveraging DRIVE AGX Thor. Crucially, production systems for DRIVE AGX Thor are now available from Tier 1 suppliers including Continental Automotive, Desay SV, Lenovo, Magna, and Quanta, signaling a move towards broader commercial deployment.

Further validating the platform's capabilities, NVIDIA-backed autonomous driving company WeRide recently unveiled its WePilot AiDrive, a Level 4 autonomous vehicle system built on NVIDIA's latest platform. This breakthrough system reportedly reduces autonomous driving costs by 50% while delivering 2,000 TOPS of AI compute power, positioning it as a significant challenger to existing full self-driving solutions.

In a very recent announcement, BlackBerry's QNX division has partnered with NVIDIA to integrate its QNX OS for Safety 8 into the DRIVE AGX Thor development kit. This integration combines NVIDIA's hardware with QNX's automotive-certified operating system, which is pre-certified to ISO 26262 ASIL-D and ISO 21434, meeting the highest functional safety and security standards in the automotive industry. This collaboration is expected to accelerate the development and deployment of safe and secure autonomous driving systems.

NVIDIA has reiterated its ambitious target of approximately $5 billion in automotive revenue for fiscal year 2026. This target implies a substantial ramp-up from the $567 million reported in Q1 FY2026, which itself represented a 72% YoY increase. The company's continued focus on enhancing its automotive offerings is also evident in the launch of NVIDIA Halos, a unified safety system for autonomous vehicles, and Cosmos Predict-2, an AI model trained on extensive real-world driving data to improve performance in challenging weather conditions.
